# Aim High Electrical Services
## Target Audience Personas

---

## Audience Overview

### Primary Audience
Homeowners in Aurora, CO and surrounding suburbs (Centennial, Parker, Highlands Ranch) who need residential electrical services — from repairs and safety inspections to modern upgrades like EV charging and smart home integration.

### Secondary Audience
Real estate agents, home inspectors, and general contractors who refer electrical work to trusted partners.

### Geographic Focus
Aurora, Colorado 80016 and surrounding Denver metro suburbs. Dense suburban housing stock, many homes built 1970s-2000s. Population ~390,000. Mix of established families, young professionals, and retirees.

## Persona 1: "The Growing Family Homeowner" — Mike & Lisa

### Demographics
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Age Range** | 35-48 |
| **Gender** | Male/Female (household decision) |
| **Location** | Aurora or Centennial, CO — suburban subdivision |
| **Income** | $85,000-$130,000 household |
| **Education** | College degree or some college |
| **Occupation** | Mid-career professionals, one may work from home |
| **Family Status** | Married with 1-3 children (elementary to high school age) |
| **Home** | 3-4 bedroom single-family, built 1985-2005, owned |

### Psychographics
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Values** | Family safety, home investment, reliability, getting things done right |
| **Interests** | Home improvement, kids' activities, neighborhood community, practical purchases |
| **Lifestyle** | Busy dual-income or single-income household, weekends spent on home and family |
| **Priorities** | Safety first, value for money, minimal disruption to family routine |

### Goals
1. Keep the family safe — no electrical hazards, code-compliant home
2. Upgrade the house for modern living (more circuits, better lighting, home office power)
3. Add value to the home for future resale
4. Finish the basement for more living space (kids' playroom, guest room)
5. Get reliable service without having to call back for the same issue

### Pain Points
1. **Aging electrical panel** — Breakers trip when running multiple appliances; worried about fire risk
2. **Can't find a trustworthy electrician** — Had bad experiences with no-shows, sloppy work, or surprise charges
3. **Basement finishing bottleneck** — Contractor says electrical needs to be done first, but finding a good electrician is hard
4. **Work-from-home power issues** — Home office setup needs dedicated circuits; current wiring can't handle it
5. **Safety anxiety** — Flickering lights, warm outlets, buzzing sounds — not sure if it's dangerous

### Objections
1. "How do I know you're actually licensed and insured?" — Need proof of credentials
2. "What's this going to cost? I don't want surprise charges." — Need transparent pricing
3. "You've only been around 3 years — what if something goes wrong later?" — Need warranty/guarantee assurance
4. "Can you work around our schedule? We have kids and can't have the power off all day." — Need flexible scheduling

### Decision Triggers
1. **Safety scare** — Tripped breaker, burning smell, flickering lights create urgency
2. **Failed home inspection** — Panel or wiring flagged during refinance or sale
3. **Renovation project** — Basement finish or kitchen remodel requires electrical first
4. **Neighbor recommendation** — Trusted referral from someone on Nextdoor or in the neighborhood
5. **Strong online reviews** — 4.9-star rating and real testimonials build confidence

### Where to Reach Them
- **Online:** Google search ("electrician near me Aurora CO"), Nextdoor, HomeAdvisor
- **Offline:** Neighborhood events, school community boards, HOA newsletters
- **Social:** Facebook (community groups), Nextdoor (primary), Instagram (secondary)
- **Referral:** From general contractors, home inspectors, real estate agents, neighbors

### Messaging That Resonates
> "Your family's safety isn't something to gamble on. Aim High Electrical brings veteran discipline and 4.9-star rated service to every job — on time, on budget, done right the first time."

**Key themes:** Safety, trust, reliability, transparent pricing, veteran values

### Demographics

| **Gender** | Male (primary), but household decision |
| **Location** | Centennial, Parker, or Highlands Ranch, CO |
| **Income** | $110,000-$180,000 household |
| **Education** | Bachelor's or Master's degree |
| **Occupation** | Tech professional, engineer, finance, or healthcare |
| **Family Status** | Married, dual-income, 0-2 young children |
| **Home** | 3-4 bedroom newer home (2000-2020), 2-3 car garage, owned |

### Psychographics
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Values** | Sustainability, technology, efficiency, quality over cheapest price |
| **Interests** | Electric vehicles, smart home tech, renewable energy, outdoor recreation |
| **Lifestyle** | Tech-forward, research-driven buyer, willing to invest in quality |
| **Priorities** | Convenience, future-proofing the home, environmental responsibility |

### Goals
1. Install a Level 2 EV charger at home (stop paying for public charging or waiting on Level 1)
2. Integrate smart home systems (lighting, thermostat, security, voice control)
3. Upgrade electrical panel to handle EV charger + future solar panels
4. Future-proof the home's electrical system for growing power demands
5. Work with someone who understands modern technology, not just old-school wiring

### Pain Points
1. **Slow home charging** — Level 1 (120V) takes 40+ hours for a full charge; needs 240V Level 2 installed
2. **Panel capacity concerns** — Not sure if existing panel can handle a 50-amp EV circuit without upgrade
3. **Finding a qualified installer** — Wants someone who specifically knows EV charger installation and permitting
4. **Permitting confusion** — Doesn't want to deal with Aurora building department; wants the electrician to handle it
5. **Future-proofing anxiety** — Wants the work done right so he doesn't have to redo it when adding solar later

### Objections
1. "Do you actually have experience with EV charger installations, or is this new for you?" — Need demonstrated expertise
2. "Will you handle the permit?" — Need full-service (not just the install)
3. "My buddy says I can do it myself — why should I hire someone?" — Need safety/code compliance argument
4. "How long will the install take? I need my garage usable." — Need clear timeline (typically 4-6 hours)

### Decision Triggers
1. **New EV purchase** — Just bought or ordered a Tesla, Rivian, or other EV
2. **Colorado tax incentives** — State EV and home charging incentives make it financially attractive
3. **Frustration with public charging** — Tired of waiting at Superchargers or paying per-kWh
4. **Home purchase** — Moving into new home, wants EV charger ready in the garage
5. **Online research** — Found Aim High via Google search for "EV charger installation Aurora CO"

### Where to Reach Them
- **Online:** Google search (high intent), EV forums/Reddit, YouTube how-to research
- **Offline:** EV owner meetups, Tesla/Rivian owner groups, tech conferences
- **Social:** Reddit (r/electricvehicles, r/Denver), LinkedIn, Instagram
- **Referral:** EV dealerships, solar installers, tech-savvy friends

### Messaging That Resonates
> "Charge at home, not at the station. Aim High installs Level 2 EV chargers with proper permitting — so your new EV is always ready when you are. Licensed, insured, veteran-owned."

**Key themes:** Expertise, convenience, permits handled, future-proofing, modern technology

| **Education** | Varies — high school to college degree |
| **Occupation** | Nearing retirement or retired; may be on fixed income |
| **Family Status** | Empty nesters, married, adult children |
| **Home** | 3-bedroom ranch or split-level, built 1975-1995, owned outright or nearly paid off |

### Psychographics
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Values** | Safety, honesty, respect, fair pricing, proven track record |
| **Interests** | Maintaining their home, gardening, grandchildren, community involvement |
| **Lifestyle** | Settled, routine-oriented, prefer to hire trusted professionals |
| **Priorities** | Safety and comfort, staying in their home, not getting overcharged |

### Goals
1. Keep the aging home safe and functional (replace outdated wiring, fix problems)
2. Install a whole-home generator for power outage peace of mind
3. Improve lighting for comfort and safety (brighter, better-placed fixtures)
4. Pass home inspection if considering selling or refinancing
5. Find ONE electrician they can trust and call whenever needed

### Pain Points
1. **Outdated and potentially hazardous wiring** — Federal Pacific or Zinsco panels (fire risk), aluminum wiring, ungrounded outlets
2. **Fear of being overcharged** — Previous bad experiences with contractors who took advantage
3. **Difficulty evaluating contractors** — Not tech-savvy enough to research online reviews easily
4. **Power outage vulnerability** — Health equipment, medication refrigeration, comfort during Colorado storms
5. **Physical limitations** — Can't do electrical work themselves anymore; need someone reliable to call

### Objections
1. "I've been burned by contractors before — how do I know I can trust you?" — Veteran-owned story + reviews are powerful here
2. "That sounds expensive — is this really necessary?" — Need clear explanation of safety risks and code requirements
3. "My home has worked fine for 40 years, why fix it now?" — Need education about aging panel risks and code changes
4. "Can someone come explain it to me in person before I decide?" — Need in-home consultation, not pushy sales
5. "Do you clean up after yourselves?" — Need assurance of respectful, clean workmanship

### Decision Triggers
1. **Power outage** — Extended outage in winter/summer creates urgency for generator
2. **Insurance or inspection requirement** — Insurer flags the panel, or inspector requires upgrade
3. **Neighbor's recommendation** — "Glen did our panel, he was wonderful" from a trusted friend
4. **Safety incident** — Sparking outlet, tripped breaker, flickering lights scare them
5. **Adult children's urging** — Kids research for parents: "Mom, you need to get that panel replaced"

### Where to Reach Them
- **Online:** Google search (simple queries), Nextdoor (neighborhood focus)
- **Offline:** Church bulletin boards, senior centers, local newspaper, direct mail
- **Social:** Facebook (primary social platform for this age group)
- **Referral:** Neighbors, church members, adult children, HVAC/plumbing contractors

### Messaging That Resonates
> "Your home has taken care of your family for decades. Now let us take care of your home. Aim High Electrical — veteran-owned, honest pricing, and a commitment to doing the job right."

**Key themes:** Trustworthiness, respect, honest pricing, safety, personal service, veteran honor
