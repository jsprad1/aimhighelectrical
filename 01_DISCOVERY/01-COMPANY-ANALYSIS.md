## SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- Veteran-owned (trust, discipline, built-in brand story)
- 4.9-star rating on HomeAdvisor (strong social proof)
- Broad service offering (9 core services — one-stop shop)
- EV charging capability (high-growth market in Colorado)
- Modern, professional landing page live on Vercel
- Free estimates (low-friction entry point for leads)
- Service area covers multiple affluent suburbs

### Weaknesses
- Limited brand awareness (only ~3 years in business vs. 20-30 year competitors)
- Single-owner dependency (business relies on Glen's availability and capacity)
- Small team may limit concurrent project capacity
- No published pricing (harder to pre-qualify leads online)
- Social media profiles not active (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn icons present but unlinked)
- No visible 24/7 emergency service guarantee
- No contact form on landing page (phone/email only)
- Google Business Profile presence unverified

### Opportunities
- **Colorado EV boom** — Massive growth in home charging installations, state incentives
- **Aging housing stock** — Aurora has many 1970s-2000s homes needing panel upgrades
- **Smart home market growth** — Increasing demand for automation and integration
- **Generator market** — Climate concerns and grid reliability driving backup power demand
- **Basement finishing trend** — Popular in Colorado to add living space and value
- **Solar integration** — Electrical work needed for residential solar panel installations
- **Local partnerships** — Network with realtors, home inspectors, general contractors
- **Content marketing** — Educational resources about electrical safety position as expert
- **Google Business Profile** — Capture local "electrician near me" search traffic
- **Veteran networks** — Military community referrals and veteran-owned business directories
- **Nextdoor platform** — Highly effective for local service recommendations in Aurora

### Threats
- Established competitors with 20+ years brand recognition and review volume
- National franchises (Mr. Electric, Mister Sparky) with marketing budgets
- Larger companies offering 24/7 emergency service
- Pricing pressure from bargain-hunting customers on lead platforms
- Economic downturn reducing home improvement spending
- Big box retailers (Home Depot, Lowe's) offering installation services
- High costs of online lead generation (HomeAdvisor/Angi fees)
- Negative online reviews disproportionately impact small businesses
- Supply chain disruptions affecting material costs and margins
- Labor shortage for qualified electricians if scaling the team
